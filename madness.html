Overall Meaning

In Ruelle's song "Madness," the lyrics express a feeling of turmoil and chaos within a person's life. The first two lines "I've seen that look in your eyes, it makes me go blind," indicate that the person is familiar with someone who exudes a sense of darkness and pain. The next line, "cut me deep, the secrets and lies," suggests that this person has hurt the singer deeply with their deceit and betrayal. The line "storm in the quiet" emphasizes that the chaos is happening internally, despite the calm exterior.


The following lines repeat the phrase "Feel the fury closing in, are we sisters wearing thin," suggesting that the internal turmoil is intensifying and causing a breaking point. The repetition of "nowhere to run from all of this havoc" emphasizes that there is no escape, leading to a sense of hopelessness. The final lines "nowhere to run, there's nowhere to hide, this is madness" convey the idea that the situation has become unbearable, and the only option is to confront and try to overcome it.


The overall message of the song is to face one's inner turmoil and try to find a way to move forward despite the chaos. It is okay to feel the anger and pain, but it is essential to acknowledge it and find a way to cope with it. In essence, the lyrics reflect on the fact that sometimes our own minds can be our greatest enemies, and we must find a way to overcome it.
