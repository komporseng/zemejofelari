Adam Gase: Professional Career

Gase’s journey started in the early 2000’ at Michigan State University, where he worked as an assistant to the coaching staff. At that time, he still was in his college, studying.

After graduating from Michigan, he joined LSU in 2000. Gase worked as a defensive assistant in the first season of his coaching career.

However, he worked full-time as recruiting staff for the next two seasons.

Adam stayed with Detroit Lions for two tears after leaving LSU. There he was promoted to the team’s quarterback coach.

Gase broke the record of John Fox in 2013. Nonetheless, 2013 was the best year for Gase in terms of his career.

And later, Miami Dolphins announced Gase as their head coach. It was the first time that Gase became a head coach.

In the first season, Gase leads his team to the semifinals. Unfortunately, Dolphins couldn’t win that season, but Gase proved to be a good head coach.

Later after Dolphins fired Gase, he joined New York Jets and made him their General Manager. The first season was, however, well, but the second season was the working in the history of the Jets.

As a result, Gase took aggressive steps as a general manager and ultimately fired Gregg Williams.

But due to constant losses, Gase was fired by the Jets in 2021.
