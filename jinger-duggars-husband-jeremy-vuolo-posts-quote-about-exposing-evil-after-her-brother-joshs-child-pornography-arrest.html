JEREMY Vuola has posted about ‘exposing evil’ ahead of disgraced brother-in-law Josh Duggar’s child pornography trial.

Taking to Twitter on Friday, Jinger Duggar’s husband shared a quote from late Christian theologian James Montgomery Boice.

The 33-year-old former soccer player wrote: “Sometimes our witness to Christ must expose and denounce evil as well as proclaim good news. To be truth, truth must reveal error, and to be good, good must expose evil.”

The new post came just a day after a similar quote Jeremy share about “godliness” on Wednesday.

He shared three photos of himself reading from the Bible as he stood at a podium speaking to a crowd.

Alongside the photos, he wrote: “Loved speaking to the kids at @campregen on 1 Timothy 4:7-8 and being an athlete in the pursuit of godliness.”

The captioned also included 1 Timothy 4:7-8, which states: “Have nothing to do with irreverent, silly myths. Rather train yourself for godliness; for while bodily training is of some value, godliness is of value in every way, as it holds promise for the present life and also for the life to come.”

Jeremy married Jinger, 27, in 2016, and the couple share daughters Felicity, three, and Evangeline, eight months.

ARREST

Jinger’s brother Josh, 33, was arrested for possession of child pornography in April and pleaded not guilty after being charged with two counts of receiving and possessing child pornography. 

He was granted bail but has restricted travel. He cannot leave Arkansas counties Benton, Washington and Madison without permission from the court until his November trial.

It was revealed this week that ahead of the trial, Josh’s pregnant wife Anna is staying with his third party custodians LaCount and Maria Reber.

Meanwhile, her family has stepped in to help raise their six kids, sources told The Sun.

COUNTING ON CANCELED

Following his arrest, The Sun revealed that the TLC reality show, Counting On, had been canceled after nearly six years.

A source told The Sun: “The decision was made by TLC earlier this week to cancel the show.

“The family was told over the phone shortly after and it’s still very hush, hush. The petition from those who boycotted Counting On was hard to ignore, and sponsors were very concerned following Josh’s arrest.”

WHOLEHEARTEDLY AGREE’

Shortly after the show’s cancellation, Jinger and Jeremy admitted the “wholeheartedly agreed” with the network’s decision.

While taking to Instagram, Jinger wrote in an emotional text: “We are very grateful for TLC giving us the opportunity to be on their network over the years and their kindness towards the Vuolo family.

“It’s been a remarkable journey that has opened doors to traveling and experiencing the world in a way that we wouldn’t have.”

NEXT CHAPTER

She continued: “We wholeheartedly agree with TLC’s decision not to renew Counting on and are excited for the next chapter in our lives.

“We’d like to thank our fans, friends, and the amazing film crew who have shown us love and support.”

The post concluded: “We look forward to continuing our creative journey in Los Angeles and seeing what the future holds.”
