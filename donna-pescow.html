Donna Pescow

Posted by Martina Birk on Thursday, July 25, 2024

Donna Pescow is an American actress and director best known for her roles in the films “Saturday Night Fever” and “Angie.” She’s also known for her roles as Donna Garland in ‘Out of This World’ and Eileen Stevens in the Disney Channel sitcom ‘Even Stevens.’

What is the Net Worth of Donna Pescow?

Early Life and Education

She was born in Brooklyn, New York, on March 24, 1954. She is 65 years old right now. Her father owned and operated a newsstand in Battery Place, but little is known about her mother.

Donna is a naturalized citizen of the United States, although her ethnicity is unknown. She graduated from Brooklyn’s Sheepshead Bay High School in 1972 and went on to the American Academy of Dramatic Arts, where she lost her Brooklyn accent. She also enrolled in Carl Weathers’ theater classes.

Career and Professional Life

In 1973, she played a prominent part in the Off-Off-Broadway musical ‘Poor Old Fool.’ Then, in 1977, she played Annette in the film “Saturday Night Fever,” for which she had to learn Brooklyn rise. After that, she was cast as Celena Arquette in the ABC soap opera “One Life to Live” in 1977. She starred in the television series ‘Angie’ from 1979 to 1980, opposite Robert Hays, Debralee Scott, and fellow Brooklynite Doris Roberts.

She played Dr. Lynn Carson on the serial opera ‘All My Children’ from 1982 to 1983, and she co-starred with Maureen Flannigan and Joe Alaskey in the comedy series ‘Out of This World’ as Donna Garland. She appeared in a number of films and television shows, including ‘Clueless,’ ‘NYPD Blue,’ ‘General Hospital,’ ‘Even Stevens,’ ‘Crossing Jordan,’ ‘The Sopranos,’ and many others.

Awards and nominations

She was nominated for a Golden Globe Award in the category Best Actress in a Television Series-Comedy or Musical for ‘Angie’ in 1980, and she was also nominated for a Daytime Emmy Award in the category Outstanding Performer in a Children’s Series for ‘Even Stevens’ in 2003.
