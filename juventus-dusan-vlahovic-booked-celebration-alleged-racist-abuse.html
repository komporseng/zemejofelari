Aston Villa target Dusan Vlahovic booked for celebration after alleged racist abuse as rival manager

Posted by Martina Birk on Friday, April 26, 2024

Juventus striker Dusan Vlahovic was left shaken after receiving a yellow card for his reaction to alleged racist abuse from Atalanta supporters.

The Serbian striker, who has been linked with a move to Aston Villa, scored in stoppage time in the Serie A clash with the Old Lady picking up a 2-0 win, but he had to deal with chanting late on from the Bergamo crowd.

‘Gypsy s***’ was heard from the home supporters, and Vlahovic looked visibly distressed as he reported the chants to referee Daniele Doveri.

Minutes later, he rifled the ball into the top corner and let out his anger during his celebration, which saw him booked by the match official.

It’s reported that Vlahovic said, 'But don't you hear them?' to the referee, with Juve commenting on the matter in their match report.

“In the end, the bad news of the day, namely the very clear chants sung by the audience towards Vlahovic,” they wrote.

“He first draws the referee's attention and then invents a fantastic goal on the counter-attack.”

The scenes were similar to Romelu Lukaku’s sending off against Juve in April, when the Belgian responded with a celebration to the home fans after being racially abused throughout.

He was given a second yellow card for excessive celebration, which was not withdrawn upon appeal.

Later, Atalanta manager Gian Piero Gasperini stormed out of a post-match interview after defending his supporters.

"Racism must be condemned but I must point out that [Mario] Pasalic plays in Atalanta and [Josip] Ilicic, or people of the same ethnic group as Vlahovic,” he said.

“Sometimes insults are due to other things and should be taken into account. Racism is a very serious matter but sometimes we confuse it. Racism concerns everyone, even our players.

“Sometimes you have to know how to distinguish it from an insult like ‘You piece of s***’ or “F*** you”. I repeat, racism would be referred to many players who are here.

“The insult is another thing and we should make this clarification. Then there is rudeness and that is yet another thing: that too should be eradicated and fought. I suffer acts of rudeness every time I go to the bench.”

A DAZN journalist then challenged the 65-year-old, saying it wasn’t abuse of an individual but their ethnic origin.

“Look, you are completely off track,” he replied. “You are very wrong to say such a thing. It's a rude chorus aimed at a single player.

“And then the boo aimed at teams that have black players? Racism must be fought but not confused. We must not make the mistake of lumping everything together because otherwise it's all racism and it's wrong.”

Gasperini then walked away.
